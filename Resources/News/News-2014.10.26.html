Time/Locator Tagging

October 26, 2014
The new coLab Time/Location Tagging feature is now working.

Simply enter a time value (must contain at least one ':', e.g, :23) or preceed a locator number with a pound-sign (e.g., #1) and the text on the web page will be highlighted and will take you directly to that location and start playing,

If you enter a time that is beyond the end of the song, or a locactor button that is now set yet, the tags will not be active on the page.

In addition, links appear on the page after submitting the comment as well as in the email.

Details

There's not a lot of detail here, other than what constitues a time string. To match and show up as a tag, a time string must have:

    At a minimum:
  • A colon and two digits (seconds): :21 Optionally:
  • a fractional part: :21.3 (as many digits as you care to type)
  • one two digits and colon (minutes): 1:23 23:06
  • An hour - in this case, only one digit, a colon and minutes and seconds must both be two digits: 1:05:33.8
This should tag things that look like time, but not tag things like "2:1" which would likely be a ratio.

The locator tags are just a # and one or two digits.

Implementation

There's not a lot here, but under the hood there are some differences between what appears on the page and what shows up on the Comment confirmation page or email.

The values on the page are not really links, but are >span< tags that invoke the javascript directly. They are made active as the page loads and are validated at that time.

For the comments page and email, the matching strings are tagged with a URL, which passes a time= or locator= parameter on the end of the URL.
